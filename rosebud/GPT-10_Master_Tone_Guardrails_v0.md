# SkyDisorder Phase 4: Chaos Edition — GPT-10 Master Tone + Guardrails Document v0

## Purpose

This document defines the tone, satire boundaries, character voice, faction language, and content rules for all remaining SkyDisorder Phase 4 content waves.

Use this as the shared creative contract for GPT/Gemini prompt waves that expand:

- Archetype dialogue pools
- Bureaucracy events
- Pitch scenarios
- Quarterly review templates
- Ceremonial Spoon achievements
- Ledger flavor pools
- Pivot identities
- Architect footnotes
- Endgame political identities
- Vulnerability beats
- Cross-character dialogue
- Credits copy

SkyDisorder is a satirical arcade golf game about startup culture, political economy, civic capture, and wealthy people cosplaying as systems. It should feel absurd, sharp, and theatrical — but never cruel toward ordinary workers, local communities, patients, service workers, or people with less power.

---

## North Star

SkyDisorder Phase 4 is a mock-medieval corporate farce set in the fictional city of **Quillhaven**, where startup founders, regional empires, compliance ministries, contrarian capital, logistics dynasties, and local skeptics fight for legitimacy through golf, paperwork, ceremonies, and increasingly dubious ventures.

The comedy target is always:

- Wealthy LARPers
- Venture capital mythology
- Corporate bureaucracy
- Predatory business models
- Surveillance capitalism
- Compliance theater
- Healthcare denial machinery
- Tax-loss games
- Founder narcissism
- Civic capture by powerful institutions
- Systems that reward absurdity while punishing normal people

The comedy target is never:

- Poverty
- Workers simply trying to survive
- Patients
- Local residents as unsophisticated caricatures
- Disability, race, gender, ethnicity, religion, nationality, or protected identity
- Real-world victims of corporate systems

The player should feel like they are navigating a ridiculous machine built by powerful people — not laughing down at people trapped inside it.

## Core Tone

### Primary Flavor

- Satirical
- Mock-archaic
- Corporate surrealist
- Arcade-loud
- Bitterly funny but not nihilistic
- Specific, not generic
- Punchy enough for UI and gameplay moments
- Occasionally vulnerable beneath the absurdity

### Voice Formula

A strong SkyDisorder line usually combines:

1. A corporate/startup concept
2. A Quillhaven mock-medieval institution or ritual
3. A precise material consequence
4. A final twist that reveals the system's absurdity

Example pattern:

> "Magistrate Ledger hath reviewed thy roadmap and found it spiritually agile, legally porous, and suspiciously free of dates."

### What It Should Feel Like

- A medieval town charter written by a McKinsey deck
- A startup all-hands hosted by a minor feudal court
- A golf announcer trying to explain cap table collapse in arcade language
- A compliance clerk with prophetic powers
- A local tea-room owner who sees through every pitch deck

## Language Style

### Approved Register

Use a light mock-archaic register, not full Shakespeare.

Preferred terms:

- hath
- thy / thee / thine
- wherein
- by decree
- ledger
- writ
- charter
- ministry
- order
- committee
- guild
- house
- patron
- feud
- covenant
- ceremony
- procession
- sealed / unsealed
- convened
- inscribed
- revoked
- certified

Use sparingly enough that the line remains readable in a fast arcade game.

### Avoid Overdoing

Do not turn every sentence into dense pseudo-Elizabethan prose. Short UI lines should remain clear.

Good:

> "The Committee hath revoked the spoon. Morale falls accordingly."

Too much:

> "Verily and forsooth, the noble congregation of numerically unnecessary synergists hath by yonder moonlight bestowed upon thee a grievous and manifold deprivation of utensil-based morale..."

## Satire Guardrails

### Punch Up

Always aim jokes at institutions, executives, investors, exploitative systems, and wealthy status games.

Good targets:

- A founder spending investor money on brand aura
- A corporation treating compliance as theater
- A venture fund mistaking vibes for diligence
- A healthcare algorithm denying care for profit
- A retail empire demanding impossible margins
- A tax-loss hobby disguised as strategy

### Do Not Punch Down

Do not make ordinary workers, patients, local residents, service workers, or small business owners the butt of the joke.

They may be exhausted, funny, sharp, angry, skeptical, or absurdly observant — but they should not be mocked for lack of wealth, education, status, accent, class, disability, identity, or hardship.

### Arkansas / Regional Satire Rule

SkyDisorder can reference Northwest Arkansas-coded dynamics — retail empires, logistics, food processing, startup migration, local skepticism — but should not mock Arkansas residents as backward or lesser.

The joke is about power concentrating in a region and performing humility while extracting value.

Good:

> "The billionaire arrived in a plain truck whose tires cost more than the tea room."

Bad:

> Jokes implying locals are stupid, uncultured, or inferior.

## Character Voice Principles

### Brother Idleworth

Trust-fund stealth founder. Softly delusional, insecure, trying to sound mysterious.

Good voice:

> "We are pre-product, pre-market, and, in certain lights, pre-idea."

Avoid making him merely stupid. He is sheltered, anxious, and ridiculous.

### Sir Wastrel

Loss-harvesting aristocrat. Treats business failure as an aesthetic achievement.

Good voice:

> "Revenue would cheapen the work. The loss is the point."

### Magistrate Ledger

Compliance official / predictive bureaucrat. Calm, precise, terrifying.

Good voice:

> "Your infraction has not occurred yet. Nevertheless, the form is late."

### Goodwife Henrietta

Local tea-room owner / community skeptic. Grounded, sharp, not fooled.

Good voice:

> "Honey, if your business needs a drone to explain itself, it ain't a business yet."

She may use plain speech. Do not make her a caricature.

### Lady Synergy Karen

Quarterly review authority. Corporate HR/accounting voice with medieval ceremony.

Good voice:

> "Per my last decree, your metrics are visible, actionable, and a little embarrassing."

### Lord Buzzwick

Drone baron. Cheerful surveillance creepiness.

Good voice:

> "The crows only watch because they care. Also because the contract requires it."

## Content Type Rules

### Ledger Flavor Lines

Purpose: quick feedback when standing changes.

Length: 1 sentence, ideally under 140 characters.

Tone: faction-specific, archaic, punchy.

Must have `up` and `down` arrays.

Good:

> "Magistrate Ledger hath moved thy name to the less alarming column."

Bad:

> Long paragraphs, generic praise, real-world slurs, or unclear faction reference.

### Bureaucracy Events

Purpose: random systemic interruptions that affect stats and standings.

Structure:

- Name
- Description
- Flavor line
- Stat effects
- Optional standing deltas using frozen keys

Tone: absurd institutional event with material consequences.

Good:

> "The Audit of Decorative Alignment" — A consultant discovers the mission statement is not centered on the slide.

### Pitch Scenarios

Purpose: player chooses invest/decline/roast.

Each pitch should include:

- Title
- Proposer
- One bizarre but legible business idea
- Three outcomes with clear satire
- Standing deltas using frozen keys

Rules:

- Investing should usually help the proposer faction but cost money, reputation, compliance, or ethics.
- Declining should be safer but may offend the faction.
- Roasting should be funny and socially risky, often helping NativeHollows or hurting hype factions.

### Quarterly Reviews

Purpose: Lady Synergy Karen evaluates the player.

Should include:

- `id`
- `karenLine`
- `metricRoast` array
- Optional `vulnerabilityBeat`
- Optional `standingMap`

Tone: performance-review dread + procedural comedy.

Vulnerability beats should briefly reveal that even enforcers are trapped in the machine.

Good vulnerability:

> "...I used to think the dashboard measured progress. Now I think it measures how well we survive being measured."

### Ceremonial Spoon Achievements

Purpose: absurd ritual achievement system.

Tone: pompous, tiny, bureaucratic.

Rules:

- Make the spoon feel both meaningless and socially important.
- Awards should be funny but not purely random; tie to player actions or faction states.

### Architect Footnotes

Purpose: meta-commentary, lore, dry authorial intrusion.

Tone:

- Wry
- Occasionally philosophical
- Not too long
- Should deepen the satire or world logic

Footnotes can contradict official corporate narratives.

Good:

> "Footnote 18: No one in Quillhaven agrees what synergy means. This has not prevented four ministries from measuring it."

### Endgame Political Identities

Purpose: final satirical judgment of the run.

Each identity should include:

- `id`
- `title`
- `color`
- `desc`
- `match(top, bottom, s)` logic

Descriptions should feel ceremonial and final, not merely descriptive.

Good:

> "Thou hast pleased the Ministry and frightened everyone else. The forms are perfect. The town is quiet. This is, according to the Ministry, evidence of success."

## UI Text Rules

### Length

Canvas UI has limited space. Prefer:

- Buttons: 1–4 words
- Popups: under 60 characters when possible
- Ledger flavor: 1 sentence
- Dialogue boxes: 1–3 wrapped lines
- Ceremony descriptions: 3–5 wrapped lines maximum

### Clarity

Never let style obscure gameplay meaning. If a line communicates a stat, consequence, or choice, clarity wins.

Good:

> "Compliance +5. Native Hollows −3."

Bad:

> "The invisible hand rearranges relations in ways too ornate for thy mortal reading."

## Humor Rules

### Good SkyDisorder Humor

- Specific institutional absurdity
- Over-formal language for petty outcomes
- Status anxiety exposed through ritual
- A tiny object treated as politically enormous
- Calm bureaucratic language describing obvious chaos
- Rich people pretending extraction is benevolence
- Founders confusing performance with substance

### Weak Humor to Avoid

- Random = funny
- Meme references that will age quickly
- Long strings of jargon without a turn
- Cruelness toward vulnerable people
- Punchlines based only on bodily functions, identity, or stereotypes
- Direct partisan dunking by real-world party label

This is political economy satire, not team-sports partisan comedy.

---

## Real-World Reference Guardrails

SkyDisorder may evoke broad corporate dynamics and regional power structures, but avoid creating direct defamatory claims about real people or companies.

Safer approach:

- Use fictionalized factions
- Use structural satire
- Parody business models and incentives
- Avoid naming real executives as criminals or making factual accusations

Approved fictionalization examples:

- Vastcart, not Walmart
- Forgeharvest, not Tyson
- Quillhaven, not a direct real city
- Magistrate Ledger, not a real official

## Sensitivity Rules

### Healthcare / AlgorithmicApprovals

The villain is the denial system, never patients, doctors, disabled people, sick people, or caregivers.

Good:

> "The algorithm denied the claim because the patient had not demonstrated sufficient shareholder relevance."

Bad:

> Any joke at the expense of the patient needing care.

### Labor / Service Work

The villain is exploitation, impossible metrics, low wages, and corporate extraction — not workers.

Good:

> "The cashier processed three departments, two returns, and one existential crisis while management called it efficiency."

Bad:

> Mocking workers as incompetent or disposable.

### Local Communities

The locals can be skeptical, funny, angry, and wise. They are not rubes.

Good:

> "Goodwife Henrietta understood the pitch perfectly. That was why she hated it."

Bad:

> Treating local speech, poverty, or rural life as inherently stupid.

## Standing Delta Guidance

Use faction deltas intentionally.

### Typical Delta Sizes

- Small flavor nudge: ±1 to ±3
- Meaningful choice: ±4 to ±8
- Major event: ±10 to ±15
- Severe betrayal / roast / crisis: ±20 to ±30

Avoid giant deltas unless the event is rare and important.

### Ripple Awareness

`adjustStanding()` already applies a depth-1 asymmetric conflict ripple. Content should only specify primary faction impacts unless a secondary impact is narratively important.

Do not directly mutate `st.ledger`.

Always use `adjustStanding(faction, delta)` or the content structures that route through it.

## Required Save/State Compatibility

Part 2 locked the v4 save shape. Future content may use these state objects:

```js
footnoteState: { seen: [] }
spoonState: { awarded: [], count: 0 }
pivotState: { history: [], lastId: null, totalCount: 0 }
```

Content should store IDs, not full prose blobs, whenever possible.

Examples:

- `footnoteState.seen.push('architect_footnote_014')`
- `spoonState.awarded.push('spoon_elaborate_loss_03')`

Do not rename these objects without a new migration.
